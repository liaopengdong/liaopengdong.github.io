How to Plan a Freelance Hourly Rate with Free Calculators

Use salary, hourly rate, billable hours, expenses, taxes, and savings calculators to estimate a realistic freelance rate.

Estimate billable hours first

A freelancer does not bill every working hour. Admin work, sales, revisions, meetings, learning, and downtime reduce billable time, so the hourly rate must cover more than direct delivery.

Include expenses and reserves

Software, hardware, insurance, taxes, unpaid time off, and emergency savings should be included in your planning. Finance and workplace calculators can help compare different income targets.

Review the rate by project type

Hourly estimates are useful even when charging fixed prices. They help reveal whether a project scope is too large, too vague, or underpriced.
